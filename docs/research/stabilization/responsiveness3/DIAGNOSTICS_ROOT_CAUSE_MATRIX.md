## Cross-cutting observations

- The single highest-leverage change is D3-1 (collapse per-item enrichment).
  Done correctly, D3-2, D3-13, D3-14 all become smaller problems.
- D3-3, D3-4, D3-10, D3-11 share one shape: extending `pollingPauseRegistry`
  to the remaining polling consumers. One sweep.
- D3-6, D3-7, D3-8 share one shape: persisting and gating Telnet capability
  discovery.
- D3-5 and D3-8 are constraints, not bugs; documenting them prevents future
  wasted optimisation effort.
- D3-13 is the only finding that has a "stop doing it" rather than "do less"
  shape. The reconciler may have a defensive purpose for crash recovery on
  Android — if so, the resume-throttle pattern preserves that intent while
  costing one storm per minute instead of one per visibility change.

## What this matrix is NOT

- It is not a list of label thresholds to loosen or warnings to suppress.
  Every row is a real cause that produces user-visible incorrect or slow
  behaviour, or a documented constraint that bounds future work.
- It is not a re-litigation of responsiveness2's matrix. Where a defect was
  closed there with code+tests+evidence, this matrix does not repeat it
  unless this stage's evidence proves the fix incomplete (D3-1 vs F-HTTP-2 is
  the only such case).
